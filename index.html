Selected Work

From measurement to design decision.

Four projects that follow the same arc: capture the body accurately, model it honestly, and turn the result into something a designer or an institution can actually use.

3D Scanning · Landmarking

Adaptive Landmark Adjustment for 3D Anthropometric Scanning

Problem
Automated landmark detection in 3D body scanning can be less reliable for muscular or non-average body shapes, which affects the accuracy of scan-derived anthropometric measurements.
Approach
Developed an adaptive landmark adjustment approach using local body geometry and anthropometric logic to refine landmark positions.
Outcome
Improved consistency of landmark placement and supported more reliable body measurement extraction.
Relevance
Reliable landmarking is essential for 3D anthropometry, sizing systems, and scan-based apparel design.
Sizing Algorithm · Military Apparel

Body Type–Based Size Recommendation Algorithm for Special Forces Uniforms

Problem
Current military uniform allocation systems rely mainly on height, chest, and waist measurements, which may not fully capture body-type variation among special forces soldiers.
Approach
Classified body types using anthropometric and fit-related data, then developed a size recommendation logic that combines key body dimensions, part-specific fit thresholds, and expert-driven decision criteria.
Outcome
A body type–based size recommendation framework for upper- and lower-body military uniform allocation, currently the subject of a Korean patent application.
Relevance
Shows how body data can be translated into a decision-support framework for institutional clothing, not just a research finding.
Sizing System · Protective Equipment

Anthropometric Sizing System for Buoyancy Bulletproof Vests

Problem
Dedicated anthropometric sizing criteria for buoyancy bulletproof vests remain limited, despite the combined demands of protection, buoyancy, and mobility.
Approach
Analyzed torso length and chest and waist circumferences from the 8th Korean Human Body Measurement Survey, examining size distributions with regression, bootstrap confidence intervals, and chi-square testing.
Outcome
A proposed sizing framework for a mixed male–female user population, supported by anthropometric distribution analysis.
Relevance
A practical path from national survey data to protective-equipment sizing criteria.
Posture Analysis · Ergonomics

Sagittal Posture Profiling for Ergonomic Apparel Design

Problem
Load-bearing conditions can alter sagittal alignment, yet posture-related fit issues are rarely reflected in how garments and gear are designed.
Approach
Extracted 2D sagittal landmarks from images of special forces soldiers to characterize posture profiles under load-bearing conditions.
Outcome
Posture profiles that support ergonomic interpretation of garment ease, articulation, and gear compatibility under load-bearing conditions.
Relevance
Links observed sagittal alignment to ergonomic considerations in apparel and tactical gear design.
Research Focus

The body is the specification.

My research sits between human body data and apparel engineering. Clothing for demanding contexts — military, protective, functional — fails quietly when it fits the average and no one else. I work on the methods that prevent that: better landmarking of 3D scans, sizing systems grounded in national anthropometric surveys, and posture analysis that reflects how bodies actually carry load.

The through-line is translation. A scan, a survey, or a posture profile is only useful once it becomes a size chart, a recommendation rule, or a pattern adjustment that a designer or an institution can act on. That last step — from data to design decision — is where my work lives.

About

Researcher first, designer always.

I am a Ph.D. student in Apparel Design and Production in the Department of Clothing and Textiles at Seoul National University. My background combines apparel design with quantitative methods — statistics, 3D scan processing, and computer-vision-based posture analysis — which lets me move between the fitting room and the dataset without losing either.

Most of my work concerns clothing where fit is not a preference but a performance requirement: uniforms, body armor, and protective equipment. I collaborate across apparel design, ergonomics, engineering, and industry-oriented research projects, with an emphasis on whether a sizing system can actually be adopted in practice.

I am also interested in how emerging tools — 3D simulation, pose estimation, and AI-assisted workflows — can expand what apparel research is able to measure, model, and translate into design.
